The Clinical Path — planned changes

Study Cards become a flashcard study tool for two things only: concept definitions and drug study. Answering every case again is removed — missed questions live in the end-of-rotation review instead.

Tap the card to flip. Concepts show the definition, mechanism, and key points. Drugs show class, MOA, use, and what to watch. Spaced-repetition style, not a quiz — no scoring, no pass/fail.

Break room, Faceoff, and Patient journeys are pulled out of the hidden “More” dropdown into one clear Extras shelf, each with a stated purpose. And every screen gets a consistent ← Back and an app-level Settings gear (top bar above).

Patient journeys

Continuity of care

Follow one fictional patient across several rotations and see how your earlier decisions carry forward — the med you started in Acute Care shows up again in the ICU.

Example: Ms. Nguyen — new AFib on apixaban (Rotation 3) → admitted with a GI bleed (Rotation 6). Your reversal & restart plan is graded against what you chose earlier.

Break room

Character & wellness

Short, low-stakes scenes with your preceptor and co-residents. They unlock as your bond grows and give the burnout/just-culture themes room to breathe.

Example: Dr. Harlin catches you skipping lunch after a rough code and talks through it — a wellness beat, no quiz.

Faceoff

Rival quiz round

A quick, timed you-vs-rival rapid round unlocked after you finish rotations — a fun, optional way to pressure-test recall.

Example: Best-of-7 rapid MCQs vs. Jamie Park. Purely optional; doesn’t affect your competency record.
