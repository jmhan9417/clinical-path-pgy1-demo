The problem

Every residency begins with a first scene.

The first weeks are overwhelming. A resident needs more than policy slides: they need a preceptor, a patient, a decision, and a reason to care about what happens next.

01

A first day with stakes

Day one drops residents into the medication-use system with a firehose of policies. Confidence — and safety habits — take months to form.

02

Learning that stays with them

Static CBT slides get clicked through, not learned. Completion ≠ competence, and everyone knows it.

03

A visible learning trail

Preceptors start rotations blind. There's no early signal of which R1–R4 goals a resident is ready for and which need coaching.

The solution

A story-driven first year that feels like a residency.

Residents learn the way preceptors actually teach — through cases, in context, with immediate feedback — inside an engaging visual novel. The experience keeps each decision connected to the skill it teaches.

1

Live the rotation

Story scenes place each skill in real clinical drama with your preceptor cast — from Badge Day to the ICU.

2

Decide on cases

Chart review, SBAR, verification queues, dosing math, code blue, journal club — real decisions, instant rationale.

3

Practice by competency area

Cases are organized across ASHP R1–R4 areas so residents and preceptors can see where practice is concentrated.

4

Review the practice record

Open a formative coaching report showing what was attempted, what needs remediation, and where to practice next.

FAQ

Questions before your first shift.

Is the clinical content reviewed?

The public demo uses guideline-referenced educational scenarios. Any institutional deployment should include licensed-pharmacist review of local policy, content, and release cadence.

How does it map to ASHP competencies?

The demo organizes learning across R1 Patient Care, R2 Practice Advancement, R3 Leadership, and R4 Teaching. The Preceptor Review is formative simulation feedback, not certification.

Does it store patient data?

No. Every patient is fictional, and the public demo saves progress locally in the browser.

Will it run in our LMS?

The public demo is a standalone web experience. LMS, SSO, and local-policy customization are future implementation work, not features of the current public demo.

Can we customize it for our hospital?

The public demo is intentionally fictional and generic. A future hospital version could layer in local policies, branding, and program-specific rotation content after pharmacist review.
