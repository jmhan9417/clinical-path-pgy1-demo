The problem

Residency orientation is a checkbox. It shouldn't be.

New residents are overwhelmed, slide-based modules don't stick, and preceptors are left with no objective evidence of where each learner actually stands.

01

Onboarding overwhelm

Day one drops residents into the medication-use system with a firehose of policies. Confidence — and safety habits — take months to form.

02

Modules nobody remembers

Static CBT slides get clicked through, not learned. Completion ≠ competence, and everyone knows it.

03

No competency evidence

Preceptors start rotations blind. There's no early signal of which R1–R4 goals a resident is ready for and which need coaching.

The solution

A story-driven first year that produces data.

Residents learn the way preceptors actually teach — through cases, in context, with immediate feedback — inside an engaging visual novel. The platform quietly records the evidence.

1

Live the rotation

Story scenes place each skill in real clinical drama with your preceptor cast — from Badge Day to the ICU.

2

Decide on cases

Chart review, SBAR, verification queues, dosing math, code blue, journal club — real decisions, instant rationale.

3

Build competency

Every correct decision fills an ASHP R1–R4 goal. Fail safely on fictional patients, not real ones.

4

Prove readiness

Export a per-resident evidence report your RPD attaches to residency documentation.

FAQ

Questions from program directors.

Is the clinical content reviewed?

Yes. Every case carries guideline references and a review field. A licensed pharmacist SME reviews and signs off the content before any customer release, and the content version is shown in the report.

How does it map to ASHP competencies?

Each case is tagged to a PGY-1 competency area and goal (R1 Patient Care, R2 Practice Advancement, R3 Leadership & Management, R4 Teaching & Education). The evidence report aggregates by goal and flags any below threshold for preceptor follow-up. It is framed as formative simulation evidence, not certification.

Does it store patient data?

No. All patients are fictional. No PHI is collected. Version 1 stores progress locally; the enterprise tier adds SSO with minimal PII (name/email) only.

Will it run in our LMS?

Yes — it ships as a SCORM 1.2 package that reports completion and score, with xAPI on the roadmap. It also runs as a standalone web app.

Can we customize it for our hospital?

Program tier includes your name, logo, brand color, and EAP/wellness contact. Health-System tier adds custom local-policy chapters and formulary swaps.
